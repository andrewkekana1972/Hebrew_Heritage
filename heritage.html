THE HEBREW HERITAGE

Chapter One

A Father, a Daughter,
and a Picture Bible

It all began with a desire to teach the Bible to my children.

Like many parents, I wanted them to know the God of the Scriptures—not simply through tradition, but through truth. I wanted their faith to be rooted in understanding, and their character to be shaped by the One who revealed Himself through the pages of the Bible.

One evening, I was lying on the floor beside my young daughter, reading aloud from a children's picture Bible. She listened attentively as I turned each page, bringing to life the stories I had grown up hearing myself.

But as I looked at the illustrations, something unsettled me.

Did the Israelites really look like this?

The question was simple, almost innocent. Yet it refused to leave me.

If these images were inaccurate, what purpose did they serve? More importantly, was I unknowingly passing on assumptions that had never been questioned?

That moment marked the beginning of a journey I never expected to take.

Questions have a way of changing
the course of a life.

What began as a father's question about a picture Bible gradually became a search for the identity of the people to whom the Bible was first given.

That search led me beyond history and archaeology, beyond theology and tradition, to something far more enduring:

language.

Nations rise and fall. Kingdoms disappear. Borders are redrawn.

But languages remember.

Language remembers.
